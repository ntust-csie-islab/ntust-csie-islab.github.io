有效率的模糊規則產生和規則的連續性驗證

摘要

在這篇論文裡，針對簡易模糊規則的產生，我們提出一個有效率的模糊類神經網路系統。在這個系統裡， 它的學習演算法成功地分析輸入資料的分佈情況，並且從訓練資料產生正確的簡易模糊規則。其中學習演 算法的推演來至Back-Propagation和Self-Organizing Map演算法的概念。這些被產生的模糊規則擁有常數 項的結論，它是非常容易計算並且推論方式非常快速。在實驗中，這個系統所產生的模糊規則在分類上有 不錯的成果。 另一方面，模糊規則的驗證上，我們提出連續性模糊規則的觀念。一般論之，模糊規則在一個系統中可能 是不協調的，如是多餘的、被包含的或是衝突的。這些不協調規則會減低系統效能並且造成不正確的結果 。連續性模糊規則的觀念是當模糊規則在前提部份彼此相交則它們在結論部份也應該相交。因而，若模糊 規則系統是一個非連續的，則存在非連續的模糊規則可能造成系統的不協調性。在這篇論文裡，我們提供 兩個例子驗證這個觀念並偵測出不協調的模糊規則，進一步修正這些系統為連續性。這些實驗的結果呈獻 出連續性系統的效能比非連續性的好。

Efficient Generation of Simplified Fuzzy Rules and Continuation Verification of Fuzzy Rules

Abstract

In this thesis, we propose an efficiency fuzzy neural network system for generating simplified fuzzy rules. In the proposed fuzzy neural network system, the revision algorithms are successful in analyzing the distribution of input data and generating perfect simplified fuzzy rules from the concepts of the Back-Propagation and the Self-Organizing Map algorithms. These generated rules are fuzzy rules with constant consequences, besides, it is easy to be calculated and the computational load of its inference is not heavy. Also, the simulation of experiments show that the generation of simplified fuzzy rules is effective in the classification problems. Also, we present the concept of fuzzy rule continuation for the verification of fuzzy rule-based systems. Generally, the rules in rule-based systems might be inconsistency, such as redundant, subsumed, or conflict. The inconsistent rules will decrease the performance and cause an incorrect result for rule-based system. The concept of fuzzy rule continuation is that if fuzzy rules intersect between the antecedents of them, then the consequences of them should intersect too. That is, if these fuzzy rule-based systems are discontinuous, then they might be inconsistent systems that have some discontinuous fuzzy rules. In this thesis, this concept is applied to two examples and these simulation results show that the outputs of continuous fuzzy systems are better than these of discontinuous systems.
